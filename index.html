What it does

School life comes with parental homework — some things due by a date (family photo by Friday, fees by the 15th), some happening on a date that needs prep (school trip Saturday, costume for talent hunt Monday). Things pile up, both parents are half-aware, someone always remembers a day too late.

SkoolBuddy is a private shared list both parents see in real time. Add a deadline once, both phones remind you the day before. No more "I thought you were handling that."

Why parents like it

Two parents, one list

Sign in with Google, share a 4-letter invite code with your co-parent. Real-time sync.

Reminders on both phones

Each phone schedules its own 8 AM ping the day before and two days before. Works offline.

Recurring deadlines

"Sports kit every Wednesday" — set once, app keeps generating future occurrences.

Calendar at a glance

See every deadline laid out on a month grid, colour-coded by tag.

Private to your family

Only the parents you explicitly invite with the share code can see your data.

No subscription. No ads.

Free to use. Built and maintained by a parent who needed it.

How to install

  1. Tap Download APK above on your Android phone. The browser saves it to Downloads.
  2. Open Files or My Files → Downloads → tap skoolbuddy.apk.
  3. Android asks how to open it. Choose Package installer (not "App market").
  4. If you see "Install blocked" or "Unknown sources", tap Settings in the popup and toggle Allow from this source for your file manager or browser. Go back and try again.
  5. Tap Install. The SkoolBuddy icon appears in your app drawer.
  6. Open SkoolBuddy → sign in with Google → add your child, or paste an invite code from your co-parent.
One more thing — keep reminders working. Long-press the SkoolBuddy icon → App info → Battery → set to Unrestricted. Without this, some Androids (Xiaomi, Oppo, Realme, OnePlus, Samsung) will silently kill the 8 AM reminders to save power.

Privacy

Your data — children's names, deadline titles, due dates — is stored privately in the cloud. Other parents can only see it if you explicitly share an invite code. We don't sell or share data with anyone, and there are no ads or third-party trackers.
